Massachusetts Academy of Math and Science at WPI

The Massachusetts Academy of Math and Science at WPI is a public magnet high school for 100 academically-accelerated juniors and seniors from Massachusetts. It was created in 1992 by the Massachusetts State Legislature to promote STEM education. Mass Academy is administered by Worcester Polytechnic Institute in Worcester, MA. All juniors take eight classes throughout the year, some of which are at a level above AP classes. Seniors take college classes at WPI, as if they were a first-year student there. Classes I chose to take include Calculus I-IV (MA 1021 - MA 1024), Introduction to Program Design (CS 1101), Object-Oriented Design Concepts (CS 2102), System Programming Concepts (CS 2303), and Machine Organization & Assembly Language (CS 2011).
